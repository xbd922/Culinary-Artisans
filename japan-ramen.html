Home >> Japan-Cuisine >> Ramen

Ramen is noodle soup consisting of wheat noodles or egg noodles, soup, meat, vegetables, and other ingredients. The soup stock(dashi) is usually made from pork bones until thick. In some areas, other ingredients such as chicken bones and seafood are also used to serve as broth. The sauce(tare) is the essence of ramen. It is made from concentrated extracts of spices from meat or fish. Some chefs will add their own secret sauce. The thick broth is combined with the sauce as seasoning to create what we all know of the ramen. Nowadays, various regions in Japan have different styles of ramen. Common ramen include soy sauce ramen, salt ramen, tonkatsu ramen, miso ramen, seafood ramen, and so on.

History of Ramen

Ramen originated from Chinese noodles soup. Since Japan was originally closed to foreign trade, and began to accept foreign tourists and traders, Chinese traders arrived in Yokohama and established Chinatown to open Chinese restaurants to attract customers. The most popular one is ramen, and ramen has become a household name. In some areas, you may notice people calling instant noodles as ramen. This is because it was invented by an entrepreneur named Momofuku Ando after he realized that ramen is a popular noodle dish. This alleviated the food scarcity faced by Japan after World War II.
