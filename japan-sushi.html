Home >> Japan >> Sushi

Sushi is a Japanese dish made with rice, cooked in vinegar, and served with raw or cooked seafood, and other ingredients as topping. Common types of sushi are sashimi, nigiri sushi, Maki, temaki, and so on. Although sashimi is a common ingredient paired with rice, people can still choose to pair it with other ingredients, such as tamagoyaki, chicken floss, cucumber, tuna, etc. Usually, people dip it in salty soy or sweet soy sauce, to give it a unique flavor.

History of Sushi

When people think of Japanese cuisine, the first thing that usually comes to mind is sushi. In fact, the origin of sushi comes from China Mainland. The sushi we know today is completely different from the original sushi, which was originally invented to prevent fish from spoiling. At the time, people used fermented rice to store fish, but discarded the rice before eating and only the fish was eaten. That is the Chinese dish called "narezushi". In the absence of a refrigerator to freeze fish, ancient people could preserve fish in this way for up to a year.

The sushi we know today is "nigiri sushi", which was invented by Mr. Hanaya Yohei, who is known as the inventor of sushi in Edomae. In the past, since it took a few months to wait for narezushi to ferment, Mr. Yohei wanted to avoid taking too long to prepare sushi. He invented nigiri sushi, which is to mix rice with vinegar to make up for the shortcomings of narezushi but still has the same flavor as narezushi.

Instruction

  1. Prepare Japanese rice
    1. Wash the rice until the water is clear
    2. Soak rice in clean water for 30 minutes
    3. Add water more than the rice
    4. Cook rice
    5. When done, mix with rice vinegar
  2. Prepare your sushi rolling mat
  3. Place a sheet of nori seaweed on the mat
  4. Place sushi rice about two thirds on top of the nori seaweed
  5. Add your fillings in a line on top of the rice in the center
  6. Roll your sushi
  7. Ready to serve
