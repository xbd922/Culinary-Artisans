Home >> Italian >> Pasta Fra Diavolo

Fra Diavolo is a spicy Italian tomato-based sauce known for its fiery heat, typically served over pasta such as spaghetti or linguine. The sauce is flavored with olive oil, garlic, and oregano, but what sets it apart is its heavy use of chilies or red pepper flakes. Seafood, particularly lobster, is commonly paired with fra diavolo, making Lobster Fra Diavolo a popular dish in Italian cuisine. Variations like Shrimp Fra Diavolo and other seafood combinations, including clams, mussels, scallops, and calamari, are also widely enjoyed.

History of Pasta Fra Diavolo

The name fra diavolo is linked to Michele Pezza, a notorious figure from 18th-century Naples who earned the nickname "Fra Diavolo," meaning "brother devil," due to his fiery temperament. Pezza survived a serious childhood illness and participated in a yearly procession dressed as a monk, where his mischievous behavior earned him the devilish moniker. As an adult, Pezza led Italian insurgents in a revolution against French occupiers. The sauce's name reflects this fiery personality, though there is no direct historical connection between Pezza and the creation of the dish itself. The association is purely in the name, symbolizing the sauce's intense heat.

Ingredient

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (or more to taste)
  • 1 spicy red pepper, chopped (such as Calabrian chili, cayenne, red serrano, red jalapeno, or Fresno pepper)
  • 14 ounces crushed tomatoes (or diced tomatoes, or fresh chopped tomatoes)
  • Several teaspoons crushed red pepper flakes (start with 1-2 teaspoons and adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
  • 6-7 fresh basil leaves, chopped

Instruction

  1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and red pepper. Cook for 5 minutes to soften.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes with their juices, red chili flakes, white wine, oregano, and salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Simmer the sauce for 10 minutes (or longer) to let the flavors develop.
  5. Stir in the parsley and basil leaves. Taste and adjust for salt.
  6. Serve with any pasta dish or seafood, like Shrimp Fra Diavolo.
  7. Heat Level: Medium. You can increase the spice with more chili flakes and hot pepper, or make a milder version by skipping the fresh pepper and using only 1 teaspoon chili flakes.
  8. Serving: This sauce pairs well with any cooked pasta and is especially perfect with seafood, such as shrimp.
  9. Storage & Leftovers: Store your Fra Diavolo Sauce in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3-4 days. Refrigerate promptly to maximize freshness.
