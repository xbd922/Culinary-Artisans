Home >> Malaysia >> Asam Laksa

Asam Laksa is a popular Malaysian dish that is known for its tangy and sour taste. It is a noodle soup that is made with a flavorful fish broth, tamarind paste, and a variety of herbs and spices. Assam laksa is a dish that is enjoyed throughout Malaysia, but it is particularly popular in the state of Penang, where it is considered a local specialty.

History of Asam Laksa

The origins of assam laksa can be traced back to the 15th century, when the region now known as Malaysia was ruled by the Malacca Sultanate. The dish is believed to have been brought to Malaysia by Chinese traders, who were known for their use of sour and tangy flavours in their cooking. Over time, the dish evolved to include local ingredients and techniques, such as the use of tamarind paste and shrimp paste.

Ingredient

  • 500g mackerel fish (ikan kembung)
  • 1 litter water
  • 200g tamarind paste
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 5 pieces dried tamarind slices (asam keping)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 5 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 3 pieces dried chilies, soaked and blended
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 stalks torch ginger flower (bunga kantan), sliced
  • 1 bunch mint leaves
  • 1 bunch polygonum leaves (daun kesum)
  • 200g rice noodles (laksa noodles)
  • Salt to taste

Instruction

  1. Prepare the Fish Broth:
    1. Boil the mackerel in water until cooked. Remove the fish, debone it, and flake the flesh. Set aside.
    2. Return the fish bones to the pot and continue boiling to make a rich broth. Strain the broth.
  2. Make the Soup Base:
    1. In the strained broth, add tamarind paste, lemongrass, dried tamarind slices, onion, garlic, and blended dried chilies.
    2. Let the mixture simmer for about 30 minutes, allowing the flavours to infuse.
    3. Add the flaked fish back into the pot, and season with salt to taste.
  3. Prepare the Noodles and Garnish:
    1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
    2. Prepare the garnishes: cucumber, red onion, torch ginger flower, mint leaves, and polygonum leaves.
  4. Assemble the Laksa:
    1. In a serving bowl, place the cooked noodles. Ladle the hot soup over the noodles.
    2. Top with the prepared garnishes. Serve hot.
