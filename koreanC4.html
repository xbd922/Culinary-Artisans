Home >> Korean >> Samgyupsal

Samgyupsal is a beloved Korean dish consisting of thick slices of pork belly that are grilled at the table. The meat is usually unseasoned, allowing the natural flavors to shine through as it crisps up on the grill. Samgyupsal is traditionally eaten wrapped in lettuce leaves with garlic, green chili, and ssamjang (a savory dipping sauce). The dish is known for its rich flavor and satisfying texture, with the crispy exterior and tender, juicy interior of the pork belly making it a favorite for many.

History of Samgyupsal

5

Samgyupsal, which translates to "three-layered flesh," refers to the three visible layers of fat in pork belly. This dish became popular in South Korea during the late 20th century as a form of social dining, where friends and family would gather around a grill to cook and eat together. It has become a staple in Korean cuisine, especially enjoyed with soju (a Korean alcoholic beverage) and a variety of side dishes. Samgyupsal is celebrated for its simplicity, as well as the communal experience of grilling and eating at the table.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds pork belly slices (about 1/4-inch thick)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Lettuce leaves, for wrapping
  • Perilla leaves, for wrapping (optional)
  • Ssamjang (a mixed dipping sauce)
  • Sliced garlic and green chili peppers
  • Kimchi
  • Steamed rice

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, sugar, minced garlic, and black pepper to create the marinade. Although Samgyupsal is traditionally unseasoned, you can marinate the pork belly for 30 minutes if you prefer a bit of added flavor.
  2. Heat a grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Place the pork belly slices on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are nicely browned and crispy. You can use kitchen scissors to cut the pork belly into bite-sized pieces as it cooks.
  3. Arrange lettuce leaves, perilla leaves, sliced garlic, green chili peppers, and ssamjang on a plate. These will be used to make the wraps.
  4. To eat, place a piece of grilled pork belly on a lettuce leaf, add a slice of garlic, a piece of green chili, and a small amount of ssamjang. Wrap it up and enjoy. Serve with kimchi and steamed rice on the side.
