Home >> Japan-Cuisine >> Gyoza

Gyoza is a dumpling made of minced meat and vegetables wrapped in dough and made with a variety of delicious fillings, such as minced pork, cabbage, etc. Gyoza are crispy on the fried side but smooth and tender on the rest, while the filling becomes moist and juicy. Serving it with rice vinegar makes the gyoza sour and juicy. After enjoying ramen or Curry Katsu, a plate of gyoza is simply delicious in the world.

History of Gyoza

Gyoza originated from mainland China and was introduced to Japan in the 19th century, but it became popular after World War II. At the time, the soldiers from China brought the Chinese fried dumpling-making method to Japan, thus becoming one of Japan's street snacks. Although the fried dumplings in Japan were introduced from mainland China, there are obvious differences between the fried dumplings and gyoza. Their fillings, cooking methods, and sizes are different.

Ingredient

  • 200g cabbage
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 spring onion
  • 30 gyoza wrappers
  • 150g minced pork meat
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp soy sauce

    • For dipping sauce:
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp la-yu chilli oil

Instruction

  1. Prepare dipping sauce
    1. Mix all the dipping sauce together, set aside
  2. Prepare the cabbage leaves
    1. Boil a few cabbage leaves until they are soft
    2. Drain and cut them into small pieces
    3. Finely slice the garlic cloves, spring onion and ginger
  3. Prepare dumpling fillings
    1. Add sliced vegetables and 150g minced pork meat to a bowl and mix with sesame oil and soy sauce
    2. Put aside for a short to let the flavors infuse
  4. Make gyoza
    1. Put 1 gyoza wrapper in palm of one hand, spoon a small amount of the mix into the center of the wrapper
    2. Dip your finger in some water and moisten the edge of the wrapper to make it seal
    3. Fold the filled gyoza wrapper in half and pinch the top to seal it
  5. Cook the gyoza
    1. Heat a little cooking oil in a pan
    2. Place the gyoza in it (flat side facing down)
    3. Cook until the bottom of the gyoza becomes golden and crispy
  6. Steam the gyoza
    1. Add a small cup of water to the pan until the gyoza are half submerged.
    2. Place a lid on top of the pan and leave the heat on medium-high
    3. To make gyoza cripsy, steam the gyoza until the water has evaporated
    4. Leave them cooking on the bottom a little longer
  7. Ready to serve.
