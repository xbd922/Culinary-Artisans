Home >> Japan >> Donburi

Donburi is a main course served with rice, made by putting various meats, fish, and vegetables in rice bowls and simmering them with sauce, which is called "don". Common types include Oyakodon(family bowl), unadon(grilled ell bowl), Gyudon(stewed beef bowl), Katsudon(pork cutlet bowl), etc. When serving, a small bowl is covered to isolate the heat from the food in the bowl and maintain the perfect temperature, making the donburi more flavorful.

History of Donburi

Its origins can be traced back to the Muromachi period(1330-1570) when it was a vegetarian dish served in temples and was called "Houhan" at the time. The word donburi can be traced back to the Edo period when a restaurant called "Kendon-ya" specialized in serving this dish. The dish was called "Lendonburi-bachi" at the time and was later shortened to "donburi". The word "Donburi" can now be abbreviated to "Don", which means big bowl in Japanese. The reason why it is as popular as ramen is that this dish was deeply loved by locals in the Edo period. It has a large portion, a delicious taste, and is easy and quick to make. People can eat the rice and toppings in one go.

Ingredient

  • Chicken thighs
  • Sake
  • Onion
  • Eggs
  • Seasonings -- Dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sugar
  • Japanese Rice

Instruction

  1. Prepare Ingredients
    1. Cut onion, chicken.
    2. Crack eggs.
  2. Steam rice
  3. Cook the onion with seasoning mixture.
  4. Add the chicken and cook until color changes.
  5. Drizzle egg mixture at 2 separate times.
  6. Spoon the prepared dish onto the steamed rice in the rice bowl.
  7. Ready to serve.
