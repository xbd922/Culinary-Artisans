Home >> Italian >> Buccellati

Buccellati cookies are a traditional Sicilian Christmas delicacy, known for their rich, spicy, and fragrant flavors. These sumptuous desserts reflect Sicily's diverse cultural influences. Typically enjoyed during December holidays, they feature a shortcrust pastry exterior with a filling of dried figs, candied zucca, chocolate, dried fruits, citron, candied orange, and raisins. While the classic buccellato is shaped like a decorated donut often displayed at the center of the table, smaller versions called buccellatini are also popular. Though traditionally a holiday treat, these cookies can be found throughout the year in Sicily.

History of Buccellati

Buccellati cookies have a rich history influenced by various cultures. The name comes from the Latin "buccellatum," referring to a donut-shaped bread made by the ancient Romans during the holidays. This Sicilian dessert shares similarities with a traditional Tuscan cake from Lucca, which has been made since the Middle Ages and also features a donut shape filled with raisins. The connection between the two regions likely began when a community from Lucca settled in Palermo, bringing their recipe to Sicily. Over time, the recipe was influenced by Arab culture, incorporating ingredients like cedars, oranges, almonds, dried figs, and cinnamon, resulting in the delicious buccellati cookies we know today.

Ingredient

  • 1 kg of flour 00
  • 250 g of lard
  • 350 g of sugar
  • 10 g of ammonia for sweets
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp of baking powder
  • 150 ml of milk
  • Citrus essence

    • Ingredients for the filling:
  • 100 g of nuts
  • 100 g of pistachios
  • 100 g of almonds
  • 100 g of hazelnuts
  • 1 tablespoon of cinnamon powder
  • 70 g of bitter cocoa powder
  • 250 g of dark chocolate
  • 120 g of candied orange peel
  • 120 g of candied citron peel
  • 250 g of orange marmalade
  • 250 g of fig jam

    • Ingredients for the glaze (optional):
  • 250 g of powdered sugar
  • 1 egg white
  • 1 lemon juice

    • Ingredients for the topping:
  • Candied cherries
  • Candied orange peel
  • Coloured sprinkles




Instruction

  1. For the shortcrust pastry, mix lard with sugar in a large bowl or mixer.
  2. Add sifted flour with yeast, then mix in eggs, essence, and ammonia dissolved in milk.
  3. Form a smooth dough, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least an hour.
  4. For the filling, blend dried fruit in a blender, chop finely but not too fine.
  5. Finely chop dark chocolate and candied peel into small cubes.
  6. Combine all filling ingredients in a bowl, mix thoroughly, cover, and refrigerate for at least an hour.
  7. Take shortcrust pastry out of the fridge, let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes, then roll it out with a rolling pin.
  8. Place the filling in the center, roll the pastry to enclose the filling, and cut off excess pastry.
  9. Slice the pastry into cookies, considering they will double in size during baking.
  10. Arrange cookies on a parchment-lined baking tray and brush with beaten egg.
  11. Bake in a preheated oven at 180Â°C for 30-40 minutes until lightly browned, then cool completely on a wire rack.
  12. For the icing, mix sifted icing sugar, egg white, salt, and lemon juice until smooth.
  13. Once cookies are completely cool, cover with icing and colored sprinkles, or brush with orange marmalade and top with rainbow sprinkles and candied cherries.
  14. Dry the cookies on a tray for at least a day before serving or packaging them as gifts. Store in a tin box for long-term storage.
