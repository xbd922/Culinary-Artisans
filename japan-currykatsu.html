Home >> Japan >> Curry Katsu

Curry Katsu made of crispy fried pork chops served in a spicy sweet curry sauce with carrots and potatoes. Chef can also cook with different meats, such as chicken breast or even ham.

History of Curry Katsu

Curry was colonized in Japan at the end of the 19th century and introduced by the British who colonized India. After that, the Japanese transformed curry and created the Japanese curry we are familiar with today. Katsu is derived from the English word "Cutlet". Curry Katsu has a special origin. In the past, there was a Western-style restaurant in Asakusa specializing in fried pork cutlets and Japanese curry. A regular customer asked the shopkeeper to add Japanese curry to the fried pork cutlets, which became what we know today as the curry katsu.

Ingredient

  • 400g cooked japanese rice
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Fukujinzuke pickled radish
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Boiled eggs

    • For curry sauce:
  • 4-5 cubes of Japanese curry
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 large potato
  • 1 carrot
  • 800ml water

    • For Katsu:
  • 80g panko breadcrumbs
  • 4tbsp plain flour
  • 4 pork tenderloin/chicken breasts
  • 1 slightly beaten egg
  • Vegetable oil for deep-frying

Instruction

  1. Prepare vegetables
    1. Cut onion, potato, and carrot into chunks
    2. put them in a large pan and fill half with water
    3. simmer until the vegetables are soft (20 minutes)
  2. Cook the rice
  3. Add Japanese curry cubes to the pot with vegetables
    1. Turn on the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
    2. Keep mixing until the curry sauce is thick and smooth.
  4. Prepare the Katsu
    1. Season the meat with salt and pepper
    2. Covering with flour and raw egg
    3. Covering with panko breadcrumbs
  5. Deep fry the katsu
    1. Cook until they become a golden brown color.
  6. Ready to serve
