Home >> Japan >> Tempura

Tempura is a fried food in which fish, meat, and vegerables are coated in flour batter and then fried. Can be used as a topping for bento or udon. Common types include chicken tempura, fried shrimp tempura, and even tempura can be user as a filling for sushi.

History of Tempura

Tempura frying technique may have arrived in Japan in the 16th century when Portuguese Catholic missionaries brought Western cooking methods, but the main style of Japanese tempura is a cuisine that originated as street food in the Edo period. In the past, various regions had different cooking methods. For example, the batter in the Kanto was made by mixing flour, eggs, and water, and the tempura was quickly fried in high-temperature sesame oil. When eating, the tempura was mixed with radish and it is eaten with mud and dipped in sauce. While in the Kansai, no eggs are used, and the tempura is fried slowly in low temperature salad oil and it is eaten with salt.

Ingredient

  • 1.75 cup water
  • 2 egg yolk
  • 1.5 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1-quart vegetable oil
  • Any vegetables or meat you want

    • For dipping sauce:
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • Some dashi granules

Instruction

  1. Mix all dipping sauce ingredients in a bowl. Set aside
  2. Mix water and egg yolk until well combined. Sift in flour and mix until the batter becomes a bit lumpy
  3. Heat vegetable oil in pan until hot but not smoking
  4. Dip ingredients one-by-one into batter and place into hot oil. Fry until the batter is golden-brown.
  5. Remove from oil and drain on a paper towel.
  6. Ready to serve
