Home >> Malaysia >> Satay

Satay is juicy, tender chicken skewers soaked in a flavorful marinade, cooked on the grill, and served with a rich and spicy peanut satay sauce.

History of Satay

Satay has a rich history that can be traced back to the vibrant street food culture of Malaysia. It is believed to have originated from Indonesian influence, particularly the Javanese kebabs. It is a dish that showcases the influence of Indonesian and Malay cooking. Over time, Malaysian chefs infused their own unique touch, resulting in the delectable satay we know today. Initially, it was popularized as a dish made with beef or chicken, but now it includes a wide range of proteins, such as lamb, pork, and even seafood.

Ingredient

    Satay:
  • 500g chicken breast or thigh, cut into small cubes
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • Skewers (soak in water if wooden)

    • Peanut Sauce:
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1-2 teaspoons red curry paste (adjust to taste)
  • Water to thin, if necessary

Instruction

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, peanut oil, turmeric, coriander, cumin, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger.
    2. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat.
    3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight, to allow the flavours to penetrate the meat.
  2. Prepare the Peanut Sauce:
    1. In a small saucepan, combine peanut butter, coconut milk, soy sauce, brown sugar, lime juice, and red curry paste.
    2. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the sauce is smooth and heated through. Add water to thin if the sauce is too thick. Set aside.
  3. Cook the Satay:
    1. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the skewers.
    2. Grill the skewers on a barbecue, grill pan, or under a broiler, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred, about 10-15 minutes.
  4. Serve:
    1. Serve the chicken satay hot with peanut sauce on the side for dipping. Optionally, serve with sliced cucumber and red onion.
